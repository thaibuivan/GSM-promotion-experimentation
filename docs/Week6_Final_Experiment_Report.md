# Final Experiment Report: 25% Voucher Promotion

**Project Context:** Causal Measurement of a 25% Discount Voucher on User Engagement
**Prepared By:** Data Science Team
**System Status:** TRUSTWORTHY (Verified via A/A Simulation)

---

## 1. Executive Summary
An A/B testing campaign was deployed targeting the `Suburban Commuters` persona to evaluate the efficacy of a 25% discount voucher. The experiment yielded statistically significant and financially viable results:
- **Primary Metric (OEC):** The voucher generated a statistically significant uplift in the average number of rides per user.
- **Financial Impact (ROI):** The incremental gross revenue generated by the additional rides fully offset the per-user voucher issuance cost ($5), resulting in a positive Return on Investment (ROI).
- **Recommendation:** Based on the conclusive data, we strongly recommend a full rollout of this campaign across the entire target segment.

---

## 2. Methodology & Target Audience Selection
To optimize marketing spend, an unsupervised learning approach (K-Means Clustering) was applied to segment the user base into distinct personas. 
- The algorithm identified four primary segments.
- Highly engaged segments (e.g., `Urban Commuters`) were excluded to prevent margin erosion.
- **Target Audience:** The `Suburban Commuters` segment (comprising 17.5% of the total user base) was selected. This cohort demonstrates high baseline potential (frequent peak-hour travel) but exhibits high recency, indicating a need for a promotional stimulus for reactivation.

---

## 3. Experiment Design
A Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) was designed to isolate the causal impact of the voucher:
- **Treatment Group (50%):** Received the 25% discount voucher.
- **Control Group (50%):** Received no promotion.
- **Overall Evaluation Criterion (OEC):** Incremental Rides.
- **Guardrail Metric:** Campaign ROI (Must be > 0).

---

## 4. Statistical & Financial Results
The experiment data was analyzed using an Independent T-Test, yielding the following insights:
- **System Integrity (Sanity Check):** Baseline covariates (`age`, `monthly_rides_history`) demonstrated perfect balance across Treatment and Control groups (P-value > 0.05), confirming successful randomization.
- **Efficacy (OEC Uplift):** The Treatment group exhibited a substantial increase in average rides. The corresponding P-value was < 0.05, confirming that the observed uplift is statistically significant and definitively attributable to the voucher rather than market variance.
- **Profitability:** The incremental revenue derived from the treatment group significantly outpaced the total cost of the campaign, resulting in a positive Net Revenue and ROI.

---

## 5. System Trustworthiness Validation
To ensure the integrity of the findings, the experimentation pipeline was subjected to rigorous stress-testing via an A/A Testing Monte Carlo simulation (1,000 iterations):
- **Sample Ratio Mismatch (SRM):** The observed SRM error rate was 4.90%, confirming unbiased traffic allocation.
- **P-Value Uniformity:** The Kolmogorov-Smirnov (KS) test confirmed that the empirical distribution of P-values was strictly uniform (False Positive Rate at 5.60%).
This validation guarantees that the experimentation engine is technically sound, and the reported uplifts are highly reliable.

---

1. **Rollout Execution:** Configure the Marketing Automation pipeline to deploy the 25% voucher to the entirety of the `Suburban Commuters` segment.
2. **Post-Launch Monitoring:** Track secondary guardrail metrics (e.g., driver cancellation rates, app uninstalls due to notification fatigue) during the initial 72 hours of the rollout.
3. **Iterative Experimentation:** Design a subsequent, tailored experiment for the next highest-potential segment (`Suburban Occasionals`).
